a. Favorite culture that you're not native to

Newari culture is a rich and vibrant tapestry of traditions, art, and social practices that has significantly shaped the historical and cultural landscape of Nepal, particularly in the Kathmandu Valley.

Newari culture is a rich and vibrant heritage of the Newar people of Nepal, characterized by its unique language, traditional arts, festivals, cuisine, and strong community ties. The Newars are the indigenous inhabitants of the Kathmandu Valley and are known for their distinctive language, Nepal Bhasa, which is part of their cultural identity. Their culture is deeply interwoven with both Hinduism and Buddhism, leading to a wide array of religious rituals and festivals throughout the year, such as Indra Jatra, Biska Jatra, and the Kumari Jatra. Art and craftsmanship are central to Newari culture, with remarkable achievements in woodcarving, metalwork, pottery, and sculpture seen in their temples, palaces, and homes. Their architecture showcases intricate designs and traditional craftsmanship that reflect centuries of cultural evolution. Newari cuisine is another important cultural element, famous for dishes like chhoyela (spiced grilled meat), yomari (sweet rice flour dumplings), and bara (lentil pancakes), often enjoyed during special occasions and festivals. Social life in Newari culture revolves around tightly-knit communities organized into caste and clan systems, which govern rituals, festivals, and social responsibilities. In summary, Newari culture is an extraordinary blend of art, religion, language, and social customs that has preserved its distinct identity in Nepal for centuries.

d. A meal that represents you

It is a staple food in Nepal and is known for its simplicity, nutritional value, and cultural significance.
